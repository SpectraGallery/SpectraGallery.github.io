⿊⾊⻄湖/The Dark Side of West Lake

"毫⽆疑问作为西湖的孩⼦，在这⼀片湖⽔边开始的⽣命记忆始终在召唤我，在梦境中浅浅吟唱。⽽那种诉说的⾯容似乎有着更久远的历史，与她今日给⼈的印象不甚相同，我似乎有某种义务将这种⾳容笑貌释放出来。这其中似乎隐隐的也和与其相 关联的⼥性神话故事种种有着无法剥离的联系、⽽这些故事让这个地⽅有别于他处 充盈着更为明显的⼥性特质，神秘、暧昧、充满爱欲、⼒量，同时伴随着⽣生不息的烟⽕⽓和仙气，我称其为⿊⾊⻄湖。

如果说⽔拥有记忆，那么也许在这⽔的暗纹下记录着她亘古⾄今的记忆吧。" - Mer

"Undoubtedly, as a child of the West Lake, the memories of a life that began by its shores always beckon me, whispering softly in my dreams. They seem to unveil a profound history, one that differs from the current perception of the lake. I feel compelled to give voice to these narratives, as if it is my duty to share their essence. There is an undeniable bond between these stories and the female myths intertwined with this place, setting it apart from others. It exudes a palpable femininity, mysterious and enigmatic, brimming with love and empowerment. The atmosphere is charged with vibrant energy and eternal enchantment, which I fondly refer to as the Black West Lake.

If water possesses memory, then perhaps its depths bear witness to the collective memories from ancient times to the present, silently etched within the dark currents of its embrace." - Mer

Black Lake

Mer comes from Hangzhou, also known as Wulin in ancient times, a name rarely mentioned in public. While Wulin nowadays is often associated with the society of martial artists, seemingly disconnected from Hangzhou, West Lake, as the iconic symbol of the city, still emphasise its dominating feminine role in Chinese culture through the legend of Madame White Snake. This tale, entwined in the conflict between love and supernatural forces, has been adapted into various forms of literature and plays, exploring themes of forbidden love, sacrifice, and the consequences of crossing boundaries between mortals and spirits. According to the myth, Madame White Snake, a white snake spirit, falls in love with a mortal man but their relationship is met with opposition from a monk. In some versions of the myth, it is said that the West Lake itself was created by the tears of Madame White Snake when she was separated from her husband and imprisoned by the monk. The lake is believed to hold her sorrow and longing.

Mer's exhibition offers a fresh perspective on viewing West Lake. Her emphasis on blackness evokes a resonance with exploring the primitive, natural, and mysterious aspects of this lake. Mer expressed that her impression of Hangzhou was saturated with water. Through her artworks, she illuminates a different facet of this watery landscape, capturing it through her unique lens, perhaps acting as a polariser that amplifies the significance of blackness.

Paintings

The exhibition commences with wave-like paintings, vibrant ripples of spectral colours. These dark hues evoke a contrasting impression of West Lake. Waves are not mere patterns, but reservoirs of energy, transferring their vitality within the surrounding space. The darkness whispers, conveying hidden messages through its colourful fluctuations.

Mer's paintings take up concepts such as nature, spontaneity, and the pursuit of harmony within different realms. The indistinct faces among the curves and the flowers can be associated with someone's true nature, following the emotional flow, and seeking personal freedom. Curves and flowers draw me to imagine the primitive and natural forces surrounding the mysterious ladies, presenting their powers and perhaps their inner voices.

The ladies in the paintings evoke my thoughts about the legend of Madame White Snake. The story remains an iconic tale deeply rooted in the cultural fabric of West Lake but few people may think about the symbolic representations of those mysterious characters or forces that hide the magical interacting field of West Lake. This symbolic strength of West Lake is what I feel from Merʼs artwork, what she called the Black West Lake, a renew respect for the mysterious power of women.

Ceramic Sculpture I

The shadow of Madame White Snake could also be found in other Merʼs artworks. The seven-layered pagoda stands as another enigmatic symbol in Mer's repertoire. In the legend of Madame White Snake, the pagoda represents the separation between mortal and spiritual realms, a means of imprisoning the white snake spirit. Mer's pagoda carries a similar connotation, symbolising the longing for a life free from constraints, a quest for individuality and authenticity. The pagodaʼs outer appearance, a deep raisin black, shrouds the inner flesh colour, symbolising humanity, vulnerability, and rawness. This stark contrast invites viewers to contemplate the duality of existence and the concealed depths within.

Lady of the Lake is an artwork which I was struggling to interpret my feeling towards. The presence of one eye is a striking element to me. It evokes me to reflect on a state of mindfulness and vigilant awareness. This singular eye represents Mer's artistic vision and her relentless pursuit of understanding the essence of reality beyond surface appearances. It urges viewers to perceive the true nature of their surroundings. However, the two pinches of fringes at the bottom of the artwork seem to indirectly evoke feelings or associations related to love and romance from two different poles, adding a touch of elegance to allure the mysterious eye.

It is worth noting that the black clays used in the Black Lake series have hints of green within them. They remind me of the role of the green snake spirit in the legend of White Shake. In various adaptations, the story highlights the enduring bond between Madame White Snake and her loyal companion, the green snake spirit. This connection adds depth to the narrative of the story, symbolising the unity and shared goals of these two female spirits. Mer's choice of raisin black echoes the presence of the green snake spirit, infusing her artworks with a mystical layer.

The artwork Yaozi (an artistic name of Merʼs friend who is also her current roommate), a combination of black and white clay, may also deliver some cooperative tendencies between two different poles. White tentacles and the black body synchronise a vivid contrast, adding some depth to the imaginary bond between the two parts.

Ceramic Sculpture II

Moving forward, the exhibition showcases Mer's ceramic fuscous brown necklace and dried lotus, each carrying its own symbolic significance. These chain ceramic works are part of the "beads' project" to which Mer has dedicated herself. When we first met, Mer shared her aspiration to create ceramic beads in Jingdezhen. The beads' project serves as her primary purpose for relocating to Jingdezhen, indicating their deep personal meaning to her. Through these two artworks, I attempt to catch a glimpse of this project.

To me, items on a chain have the ability to express inner emotions and encapsulate fleeting moments. They act as conduits, connecting past and present, and triggering profound emotions. The collection of beads and dried lotus leaves symbolise unity, collaboration, and the strength that emerges when diverse individuals come together. Each distinct item, whether a stone or a hand gesture, celebrates diversity and individuality while highlighting the importance of non-verbal communication and self-expression.

The artwork entitled "Stairs to an unknown destination" may reflect Mer's personal change or spiritual growth. Stairs are commonly associated with the idea of progress, advancement, or growth, signifying the process of overcoming challenges. Mer's journey to Jingdezhen can be seen as a transition or passage from one state to another. The crossing of thresholds in stairs can evoke the notion of her personal evolution or transition in this journey. Of course, stairs also imply hierarchies and orders, where each step represents a different level of authority. Mer may convey her understanding of the social structure and power dynamics as she ascends the dark stair.

Ceramic Utensils and Others

Lastly, the exhibition features a range of tableware, including boat-shaped containers, drinking vessels, bowls, and spoons. These carefully crafted utensils hold personal significance for Mer, as they evoke memories of her grandfather and her deep appreciation for wine and cuisine. The choice of specific colours and arrangements reflects her emotional state, eliciting various feelings and sentiments within the viewer.

In conclusion, Mer's exhibition invites us to explore her perspective on the Lake and depicts a mystical and multifaceted world that imaginarily exists on the dark side, where nature, spirituality, and personal transformation converge. Through her paintings, ceramic sculpture, and utensils, Mer invites us to delve deeper into the layers of meaning and emotions embedded in the feminine symbolic realm.

Her exhibition not only presents an alternative perspective on the strength inherent in water but also serves as a tribute to the enduring symbolism of water. It reminds us of the timeless power of continuity, indivisibility, and flexibility.

As I immerse myself in Mer's artworks, I am reminded of the significance of introspection and the pursuit of truth beyond surface appearances. The exhibition encourages viewers to contemplate the duality of existence, the interconnectedness of beings, and the transformative power of personal growth.

The exhibition serves as an invitation to step beyond the boundaries of the known, to embrace the mystery and power of the Lake, and to find solace in the ever-changing currents of the feminine role. It is a testament to Mer's artistic vision and personal exploration, inviting us to join her in this journey of self-discovery and connection. Mer's artistic journey showcases her unwavering its dedication to capturing fleeting moments, evoking emotions, and connecting with her innermost self. Each artwork is a vessel carrying memories, energies, and the invisible threads that bind us to the past and shape our present.

Some "past," forever alive, resists its demise. It is not merely a passage, but an eternal disguise. Through change, one alters its essence and revises.
